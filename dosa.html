Dosa and Sambar Recipe

Introduction

Dosa and Sambar are classic South Indian dishes that together form a delightful and nutritious meal. Dosa, a thin and crispy fermented pancake made from rice and urad dal, pairs perfectly with Sambar, a flavorful lentil-based vegetable stew enriched with aromatic spices.

Ingredients

Dosa:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste

Sambar:

  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, potatoes, beans, etc.)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup tamarind extract
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Dosa:

  1. Rinse and soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds for 6-8 hours.
  2. Blend into a smooth batter, add salt, and ferment overnight.
  3. Heat a griddle, pour a ladle of batter, and spread it into a thin circle. Cook until golden brown.

Sambar:

  1. Cook toor dal with vegetables until soft. Mash the dal and set aside.
  2. In a pan, heat oil, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, hing, and curry leaves.
  3. Add chopped onions, tomatoes, and saut√© until they turn soft.
  4. Add tamarind extract, sambar powder, salt, and cooked dal-vegetable mixture. Simmer until flavors blend.
